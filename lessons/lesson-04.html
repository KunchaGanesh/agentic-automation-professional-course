Applying Context Grounding for Secure, Accurate AI

The Importance of Trustworthy AI Outputs

Context Grounding is part of the UiPath AI Trust Layer and allows you to bring your data to generate more accurate, reliable AI predictions by grounding prompts with relevant business information before they reach the LLM. This approach reduces hallucination risk through references to ground truth data and helps overcome

context window limitations that can degrade model accuracy. Beyond data accuracy, guardrails provide built-in safety and quality checks at agent, LLM, and tool levels— automatically detecting and blocking problematic behavior while maintaining compliance and improving overall trust in automated decision-making. Best practices include validating inputs, applying the least-context principle, completing tool descriptions with guardrails, and ensuring normalized tool outputs—all critical to scaling agentic automation safely.

Context grounding is the process of connecting AI agents to your business-specific data, ensuring their responses are based on real, up-to-date information—not just what a general language model "knows." Without grounding, large language models (LLMs) may produce hallucinations: plausible-sounding but incorrect or irrelevant answers. By grounding AI in enterprise knowledge, you dramatically reduce these risks and enable your agents to deliver results you can trust.

Key Benefits of Context Grounding

Explore the key benefits of context grounding for agentic automation. Expand each section to see how context grounding supports accuracy, compliance, and more.

Accuracy

Context grounding reduces hallucinations and errors by anchoring AI outputs in real business data. This ensures that responses are not just plausible, but actually correct and relevant to your organization. Agents reference your approved sources, so their answers reflect the latest and most accurate information available.

Relevance

By connecting to your organization’s current data, context grounding ensures that AI responses are tailored to your specific needs and environment. This means agents can adapt to changes in your business, policies, or customer requirements in real time.

Compliance

Context grounding supports regulatory requirements by ensuring AI only references approved and compliant data sources. This is essential for industries like healthcare or finance, where using the wrong data can lead to serious consequences.

Transparency

With context grounding, AI-generated outputs include evidence and citations from your business data. This transparency allows users to verify answers and builds trust in automated decisions.

Security

Context grounding leverages permissioned indexes and access controls to protect sensitive information. Only authorized users and agents can access specific data, supporting robust data security and governance.

How Context Grounding Works in UiPath

Context Grounding starts by creating indexes and embeddings of your business data in a UiPath-managed vector database, optimizing data using extraction, chunking,

retrieval, and re-ranking techniques. When an AI agent receives a prompt, it interprets it as a query and searches through these embeddings using cosine similarity search to find the most relevant information before RAG execution. The LLM then grounds its response by augmenting the original prompt with this most relevant context, ensuring generations are more specific and tailored to your data. By default, Context Grounding provides citations—or proof of knowledge—from which the generation was based, so you can verify and validate the source of every answer. You can access the Trace view in agent runs to see all search results and citations from the Context Grounding query, offering full insights into the agent's decision-making process.

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) and

the UiPath AI Trust Layer

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) is the backbone of context grounding in UiPath. RAG retrieves relevant information from your business data and augments AI prompts, so the AI’s responses are always based on real, verifiable evidence. The UiPath AI Trust Layer orchestrates this process, ensuring that every AI output is grounded, secure, and auditable.

Main Components and Flow of RAG in UiPath

Expand each tab to explore how Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) works in UiPath, from formulating queries to providing transparent, evidence-based responses.

The agent begins by formulating a question or prompt, often using business context or user input. This query is designed to extract the most relevant information from your indexed data. For example, a user might ask for the latest sales figures, and the agent translates this into a structured query that can be matched against your business data.

Once the query is ready, the agent searches the indexes for the most relevant data. This process uses advanced techniques like semantic search and re-ranking to ensure the best matches are found. Retrieval ensures that only the most accurate and up-to-date information is considered for the AI’s response, filtering out irrelevant or outdated data.

After retrieving the relevant data, the agent augments the original prompt with this evidence. This step is what "grounds" the AI’s response in real business knowledge. Grounding helps prevent hallucinations by ensuring the AI can only answer based on the information it has actually found in your data sources.

With the prompt now grounded in evidence, the AI generates a response. This output is more accurate and reliable because it is directly linked to your business data. The generation step leverages the power of large language models, but always within the boundaries set by your organization’s knowledge and context.

Finally, the AI provides citations—references to the specific data sources used to support its answer. This transparency builds trust and allows users to verify the information. Citations are especially important in regulated industries, where traceability and auditability are required for compliance.

Integrating business data into AI workflows is essential for delivering accurate and relevant automation. In UiPath, this is achieved by creating indexes from your business documents—such as PDFs, spreadsheets, or text files—which are then securely stored and managed. Agents can access these indexes based on permissions, ensuring that only authorized data is used for grounding responses.

Supported data formats include CSV, DOCX, JSON, PDF, TXT, and XLSX, making it easy to bring a wide range of business knowledge into your AI workflows. Keeping these indexes updated and synchronized with your source data is crucial for maintaining accuracy and relevance over time.

Lifecycle of a Context-Grounded Query

When a user submits a query to an agent, the entire process operates within a secure, governed framework. Context Grounding is tenant-scoped and takes advantage of existing RBAC and AuthZ policies in UiPath, encrypting data at rest and in transit, with no data shared across indices within the same tenant or across tenants. The system enforces folder-level authorization in Orchestrator buckets, leveraging existing authentication and Automation Ops policies to ensure only authorized users can access specific data indexes. Once access is verified, the agent retrieves the most relevant information from indexed business data using extraction, chunking, retrieval, and re-ranking techniques optimized for different data formats, then grounds the AI's response with this context before LLM execution. Throughout execution, guardrails monitor requests and responses exchanged between the agent and the language model, validating tool inputs and outputs to automatically detect and block problematic behavior while maintaining compliance. The AI Trust Layer provides comprehensive audit logging that captures details of all LLM interactions for traceability, compliance, and debugging, with usage tracking per tenant, service, and model. Every operation—including what data was accessed, when, by whom, and how it was processed—is recorded in audit logs accessible for the last 60 days, supporting regulatory compliance and governance requirements. The final response includes proof of knowledge citations that cite the reference source and text from semantic similarity search results, and the Trace view provides all search results and citations from the Context Grounding query, offering complete insights into the agent's decision-making process. This end-to-end transparency—from secure query submission through secure data retrieval, evidence-grounded response generation, and comprehensive audit trails—ensures that only authorized data is used, all actions are tracked for compliance, and every answer can be verified against its source, building stakeholder trust and supporting governance requirements.

Best Practices for Secure, Compliant, and Effective Context Grounding

Explore these best practices to ensure your context grounding implementation is secure, compliant, and delivers maximum value.

Security Controls

Use tenant-scoped indexes, versioning, and granular access permissions to protect sensitive business data. This ensures only authorized users and agents can access specific information. Regularly review and update permissions as your organization’s needs evolve.

Compliance

Leverage features like HIPAA and FedRAMP support, audit trails, and regulatory alignment to meet industry requirements. These controls help you demonstrate compliance and maintain trust with stakeholders. Maintain detailed logs of data access and usage for auditing and reporting purposes.

Data Governance

Manage the lifecycle of your indexes by keeping them updated, syncing data regularly, and ensuring data freshness. Good governance practices help prevent outdated or incorrect information from being used in AI responses. Establish clear processes for index creation, deletion, and maintenance.

Operational Limits

Be aware of index limits, supported file types, and risk reduction strategies. Plan your ingestion workflows to normalize and chunk large or scanned documents for optimal retrieval. Monitor system performance and adjust configurations as your data volumes grow.

Transparency

Always provide citations with AI-generated outputs and create feedback loops for verification. This allows users to validate responses and continuously improve the quality of your AI automation. Encourage users to report discrepancies, helping refine and strengthen your context grounding approach.

Grounded AI is trustworthy AI—ensure your agents always reference the right data, at the right time.
