Integrating Agents with Enterprise Systems and Context

Integrating Agentic Automation Across the Enterprise

Agentic automation delivers the greatest value when agents, robots, people, enterprise applications, and business data work together as a unified system. Enterprise integration enables agents to access information, interact with business applications, trigger automations, and participate in end-to-end processes across organizational boundaries

In this lesson, you'll discover how robust integration strategies empower agents to deliver smarter, faster, and more reliable outcomes by connecting with the systems and knowledge that drive your organization.

This lesson focuses on developing integration skills essential for connecting agents with enterprise systems and enhancing business processes. You will explore various strategies and tools to achieve seamless integration and automation.

What you’ll learn

  1. Explore approaches for integrating agents with enterprise systems.
  2. Learn to leverage context and indexes for business knowledge.
  3. Understand API and automation tool integration.
  4. Design hybrid agentic and RPA architectures.

Integration is a critical success factor for agentic automation because agents rely on timely, accurate access to enterprise data, applications, and automations to make intelligent decisions. Without strong integration, even the most advanced agent can be limited by siloed data, inconsistent access, or outdated information.

Common challenges include connecting to legacy systems that lack modern APIs, managing data spread across multiple repositories, and ensuring secure, permissioned access to sensitive business knowledge. Agentic automation addresses

these hurdles by providing flexible integration options that bridge the gap between new AI-powered solutions and existing enterprise infrastructure.

Approaches to Agent Integration

Explore the main approaches for integrating agents with enterprise systems and data sources. Each method offers unique strengths and is suited to different scenarios within your organization.

Direct API Integration

Agents can interact with enterprise applications and external services through APIs, enabling real-time access to business data and the execution of actions across systems. Using UiPath Integration Service and API-based integrations, agents can retrieve information, update records, trigger processes, and exchange data with business applications. Common use cases include: Retrieving customer information from a CRM system. Updating records in ERP or business applications. Accessing external services and third-party platforms. Triggering workflows and business processes based on agent decisions.

Example:

An agent receives a customer inquiry, retrieves account information from a CRM system through an API, and then initiates a follow-up process to update customer records or create a service request.

Best Practice:

Use secure authentication methods, follow organizational governance policies, and maintain clear documentation for integrations to ensure reliability, security, and long-term maintainability.

RPA Tool Integration

Agents can work alongside existing UiPath automations to perform actions in applications and systems that may not provide APIs or modern integration options. By invoking robots and automated workflows, agents can extend their capabilities beyond information retrieval and decision-making to execute business tasks across enterprise systems. Common use cases include: Interacting with legacy applications through user interfaces. Extracting data from desktop applications and mainframe systems. Processing documents using document understanding and automation workflows. Updating records across multiple business systems. Executing end-to-end business processes that combine agent decisions with robotic execution.

Example:

An agent determines that a customer address needs to be updated. Instead of calling an API, it triggers a UiPath automation that logs into a legacy application, navigates the user interface, and updates the customer record.

Best Practice:

Reuse existing, tested UiPath automations whenever possible. Leveraging proven automation components reduces development effort, improves reliability, and accelerates the adoption of agentic automation across the enterprise.

Context Indexes

Agents can access enterprise knowledge through Knowledge Indexes/Context Grounding. Retrieved information is used to ground responses and support reasoning. Use cases include retrieving policies, procedures, and other business documents.

Maintaining current content and providing clear index descriptions is consistent with documented guidance.

Document & Data Repository Integration

Agents can access information stored in enterprise document repositories and business data sources through UiPath integrations and connected knowledge sources. This enables agents to use both structured and unstructured organizational data when retrieving information, answering questions, and supporting business processes. Common sources include: Enterprise document repositories Cloud storage platforms Business applications and databases Knowledge bases and internal documentation

Example:

An agent can retrieve information from company policies, procedures, and business documents stored in connected repositories and use that information to provide grounded, context-aware responses.

Best Practice:

Use approved integrations and connectors to ensure secure access to enterprise data, maintain governance requirements, and keep knowledge sources synchronized and up to date.

Agentic Automation Integration Landscape

In an agentic automation architecture, agents operate alongside robots, enterprise applications, business data, and knowledge sources to support end-to-end business processes. Agents can access information through Knowledge Indexes, interact with enterprise systems through integrations and APIs, and invoke automations to perform actions across applications.

A typical architecture includes:

Agents for reasoning, decision-making, and conversational interactions. Enterprise applications and APIs for accessing and updating business data. UiPath automations and robots for executing tasks and interacting with systems. Knowledge Indexes (Context Grounding) for retrieving organization-specific information.

Business data and connected systems that provide the context required for informed decisions.

Together, these components enable agents, robots, and people to participate in coordinated business processes, helping organizations automate work across multiple systems while maintaining governance and control.

Knowledge Indexes and Context Grounding

Knowledge Indexes enable agents to access organization-specific information and use it as context when generating responses. By retrieving relevant content from trusted business sources, agents can provide grounded answers that reflect current enterprise knowledge rather than relying solely on a language model's general knowledge.

To maintain retrieval quality:

Keep source content current and accurate. Synchronize and update indexes when business information changes. Use clear, descriptive index names and descriptions. Regularly review retrieval behavior and agent responses to ensure relevance.

Well-maintained Knowledge Indexes help agents deliver more accurate, reliable, and business-relevant outcomes.

Scenario: Combining Agentic Automation with Traditional RPA

Imagine a business process—like customer onboarding or insurance claims processing—that requires coordination across modern cloud systems, legacy

applications, and human approvals. In these scenarios, agents can act as intelligent orchestrators, connecting APIs, RPA bots, and business knowledge to automate complex, cross-system workflows from end to end.

Introduction to Hybrid Agentic and RPA Workflow

This process outlines how an agent can coordinate with APIs, RPA bots, and human users to automate a complex business workflow.

  1. 1
    Agent Receives Goal & Context
    The agent is triggered by a business event and receives relevant context from indexes, such as customer details or policy rules.
  2. 2
    API Data Retrieval
    The agent fetches structured data from enterprise systems using direct API integration, ensuring it has the latest information.
  3. 3
    RPA Bot Execution
    The agent triggers a UiPath RPA bot to interact with a legacy application or perform UIbased tasks that cannot be automated via API.
  4. 4
    Contextual Decision-Making
    The agent analyzes all gathered data and business knowledge to make informed decisions or generate responses.
  5. 5
    Human-in-the-Loop (if needed) If an exception or approval is required, the agent escalates the case to a human user for review or confirmation.
    Step 6

Outcome Delivery

The agent compiles the results, updates records in relevant systems, and communicates outcomes to stakeholders.

Best Practices for Agent Integration

Explore these best practices to ensure your agent integrations are secure, scalable, and effective. Each tip is paired with a common pitfall to avoid.

Plan for Data Freshness

Regularly update indexes and verify API connections to ensure agents always work with current information. Pitfall: Relying on outdated data can lead to incorrect decisions or compliance issues.

Enforce Security & Permissions

Implement folder-level permissions and strict access controls for all integrations. Pitfall: Overly broad permissions increase the risk of data breaches or unauthorized access.

Design for Modularity

Build agents, bots, and integrations as reusable components that can be easily updated or replaced. Pitfall: Monolithic designs make maintenance and scaling difficult.

Monitor and Audit

Regularly monitor agent executions, automations, integrations, and retrieval activities to ensure reliable operation. Use available monitoring, logging, and traceability features to investigate issues, review execution behavior, and validate that processes are performing as expected.

Pitfall: Without effective monitoring and review, execution failures, retrieval issues, or integration problems may go undetected, reducing the reliability and effectiveness of automated processes.

Leverage Feedback Loops

Regularly review agent responses, process outcomes, and user feedback to identify opportunities for improvement. Use these insights to refine agent instructions, update Knowledge Indexes, improve integrations, and optimize business processes. Pitfall: Failing to review feedback and operational results can allow retrieval gaps, inaccurate responses, or process inefficiencies to persist over time, reducing the effectiveness of agentic automation.

The future of enterprise automation is hybrid—combining agentic intelligence with robust system integration to achieve maximum impact.
