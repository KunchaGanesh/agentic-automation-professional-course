Introduction to Agentic Automation and UiPath Agent Types

Welcome to Agentic Automation: The Next Evolution in Intelligent Automation

The world of automation is changing faster than ever. As organizations strive for greater efficiency and adaptability, traditional rule-based bots are no longer enough. Agentic automation represents a major leap forward, enabling businesses to deploy intelligent, goal-driven agents that can plan, adapt, and learn. In this lesson, you'll discover how agentic automation is transforming the way automation professionals design, build, and manage solutions—and why it's essential for staying ahead in today's digital landscape.

This lesson will help you understand the fundamentals of agentic automation and its unique characteristics. By mastering these concepts, you'll be equipped to identify different agent types and appreciate the importance of the agent lifecycle in automation.

What you’ll learn

  1. Define agentic automation and its significance.
  2. Explain the core principles that set agentic automation apart.
  3. Distinguish between supervised, autonomous, and conversational agent types.
  4. Describe the agent lifecycle and its importance in reliable automation.

Agentic automation is a new paradigm in enterprise automation, moving beyond traditional robotic process automation (RPA) that executes predefined tasks. Instead, agentic automation leverages AI-powered agents that are driven by goals, not just step-by-step instructions. These agents can plan, make decisions, and adapt to changing environments, making them far more flexible and intelligent than classic bots.

Unlike traditional bots, which often struggle with complex, dynamic scenarios, agentic automation empowers agents to understand objectives, interact with multiple

systems, and continuously improve through learning. For example, while a simple bot might extract data from invoices using fixed rules, an agentic solution could handle exceptions, learn from new document formats, and escalate issues to humans only when necessary. This shift enables organizations to automate more sophisticated workflows and deliver greater business value.

Evolution of Automation: From Bots to Agents

Automation has evolved through distinct stages, each building on previous innovations to deliver greater sophistication and capability. RPA robots emerged as the foundation, automating high-volume, repetitive tasks through rule-based logic and fixed workflows—delivering efficiency gains in structured processes like data entry, invoice processing, and transaction handling.

As business needs grew more complex, AI integration entered the picture, bringing machine learning and large language models into the automation mix. Today, agents

represent the next evolution: AI-powered entities designed to operate in dynamic, unstructured environments where traditional RPA struggles. Unlike deterministic RPA robots that follow predefined rules, agents use a probabilistic approach to plan, act, learn, and adapt in real time based on patterns and business context. Agentic automation orchestrates agents, robots, and humans in a unified ecosystem, combining the strengths of each.

AI agents can trigger RPA bots as tools, escalate decisions to humans when judgment is needed, and continuously improve through feedback. This multi-layered approach enables organizations to automate previously unreachable areas—exception-heavy workflows, judgment-based processes, and complex decision scenarios—while maintaining human oversight and control.

Core Principles of Agentic Automation

Explore the foundational principles that make agentic automation unique and powerful. Each principle contributes to the intelligence, flexibility, and reliability of AI-powered agents in enterprise environments.

Planning

Agents set goals and create action plans to achieve them, rather than simply following a list of tasks. This allows them to operate with purpose and direction, adapting their actions as needed.

Tool Usage

Agents leverage a wide range of tools, including RPA automations, APIs, and integrations, to complete complex tasks efficiently. Their ability to select and use the right tools is key to their effectiveness.

Context Awareness

Agents use business context and filter out irrelevant or inaccurate information (such as hallucinations from language models) to ensure their actions are accurate and relevant. This keeps automation trustworthy and aligned with business needs.

Adaptability

Agents process real-time information from their environment, allowing them to stay interactive and responsive. This adaptability makes them suitable for dynamic, ever-changing business scenarios.

Reflection & Memory

Agents learn from past actions, tool usage, and interactions. By reflecting on their behavior, they continuously optimize their responses and improve over time.

The point of an 'agent' is that you give it goals—not tasks.

UiPath Agent Types: Explore the Differences

Expand each tab to see how different UiPath agent types operate and when to use them.

Supervised agents are designed for scenarios where human oversight is essential. These agents operate through conversational interfaces, requiring user confirmation before each action is taken. This makes them ideal for ad hoc tasks or processes where adaptability and control are critical. For example, a supervised agent might assist a finance team by suggesting actions for invoice approvals, but waits for a human to confirm each step before proceeding. This ensures accuracy and compliance in sensitive workflows.

Autonomous agents are built to execute end-to-end workflows independently, based on clearly defined goals. Once triggered, they operate with minimal human intervention, escalating to a human only if intervention or assessment is required. For instance, an autonomous agent could process large batches of structured documents, generate reports, or perform data transformations without ongoing user input. This makes them ideal for high-volume, rule-based, or repetitive operations.

Conversational agents specialize in real-time, multi-turn dialogue with users. They maintain context across messages, adapt responses dynamically, and can execute automations or escalate to humans as needed. These agents are perfect for support, guidance, and interactive automation scenarios. Imagine an HR onboarding assistant that guides new employees through forms, answers questions, and provides personalized support—all through a natural language interface.

Conversational agents excel in delivering human-like engagement and seamless user experiences.

How Agents Are Designed, Evaluated, and Managed

Building reliable agentic automation requires more than just choosing the right agent type. The agent lifecycle is a structured process that guides you from initial design through evaluation, deployment, and continuous monitoring. Understanding this lifecycle is crucial for delivering automation solutions that are robust, scalable, and aligned with business goals.

Introduction to the UiPath Agent Lifecycle

Let’s walk through the key stages in the lifecycle of a UiPath agent.

  1. 1
    Design
    Define the agent’s persona, goals, required tools, and guardrails for safe operation. Set up conversation flows and escalation paths for human handover if needed.
  2. 2
    Evaluation
    Test the agent’s conversations and actions using real business scenarios. Measure accuracy, relevance, and tool execution performance, validating both typical and edge cases.
  3. 3
    Deployment
    Publish the agent to Orchestrator, embed it in applications like Teams or Slack, and ensure governance controls are in place for secure access.
  4. 4
    Monitor
    Track agent usage and performance through dashboards and logs. Collect user feedback and continuously refine the agent’s behavior to improve outcomes.

Summary of the Agent Lifecycle

Following the agent lifecycle ensures your automation solutions remain effective, reliable, and aligned with evolving business needs.
